I registered the seth.cool domain in 2016 with no project in mind – only the misguided assumptions that someone else would buy it, and that I’m cool. A couple years later, I decided to use my first-mover’s advantage to share data-related projects as I worked on them.

At the time, data analysis was a relatively new interest of mine. While working on the customer support and renewals teams at Discovery Education, I noticed that when I wasn’t communicating with customers, I was spending the majority of my time working with pseudo-CRMs1 in Excel. The data grew larger (in relative spreadsheet terms), and I complained to a friend about my laptop freezing every time I ran VLOOKUPs, or tried to filter on a column. He suggested that I learn to use R, and recommended a few books2 for me to get started.

As I learned the basics of data manipulating in R with the help of several amazing resources3, I realized I wanted to shift my professional focus from customer-facing to data-facing. After six months of learning, I felt I had the competency to do junior-level-ish data work, but no good way to prove it. In retrospect, I was lucky to have a manager who valued my Excel jockeying, and a year later, advocated for me to leave her team and join one that allowed me to focus on data. Before that lucky break, I discovered Yihui Xie’s excellent blogdown R package, and decided that my best option for proving my new-found data competencies would be to start a data analysis blog, completely powered by R.

At some point, I plan to write a dedicated post detailing my somewhat unconventional experiences becoming a professional data practitioner.
